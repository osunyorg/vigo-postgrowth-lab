On November 21st, the ATTRACT project participated in the Experiences Panel held at the Faculty of Telecommunications Engineering of the University of Vigo during the 6th Conference on Technology for the Common Good (VI Xornada de Tecnoloxía para o Ben Común -Educación, investigación e cooperación para o desenvolvemento nas universidades galegas). The presentation highlighted ATTRACT's work on the Right to Repair (R2R), emphasizing its relevance to sustainable development and social justice.    

body: >-


ATTRACT was presented at the Experiences Panel of
the 6th Conference on Technology for the Common Good - Cooperation in
Education, Research and Development at Galician Universities (VI Xornada de
Tecnoloxía para o Ben Común -Educación, investigación e cooperación para o
desenvolvemento nas universidades galegas). This event was organized at
the Faculty of Telecommunications Engineering of the University of Vigo on
November 21st.

The presentation focused on
the Right to Repair as a key socio-technical issue, connecting academic
research, educational practices, and development cooperation. Through ATTRACT,
the project demonstrated how the Right to Repair can foster more sustainable,
inclusive, and democratic technological approaches, reinforcing the role of
universities in promoting the common good.

ATTRACT was presented at the Experiences Panel of the 6th Conference on Technology for the Common Good - Cooperation in Education, Research and Development at Galician Universities (VI Xornada de Tecnoloxía para o Ben Común -Educación, investigación e cooperación para o desenvolvemento nas universidades galegas). This event was organized at the Faculty of Telecommunications Engineering of the University of Vigo on November 21st. The presentation focused on the Right to Repair as a key socio-technical issue, connecting academic research, educational practices, and development cooperation. Through ATTRACT, the project demonstrated how the Right to Repair can foster more sustainable, inclusive, and democratic technological approaches, reinforcing the role of universities in promoting the common good.
