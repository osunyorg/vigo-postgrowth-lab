"The time of the finite world begins." - Paul Valéry   As the planetary boundaries breaches one after another, marketing remains deeply rooted in a logic of growth, market expansion, consumerism and demand stimulation that are increasingly incompatible with a world destabilized by the Anthropocene. Despite a growing body of academic work calling for the adaptation of marketing to sustainability imperatives, actual organizational practices remain largely anchored in a paradigm of weak sustainability insufficient to meet the criticality of the challenges we now face.   This raises the following question: Can marketing survive the breaching of planetary boundaries? This in-progress research explores a longitudinal single case study of a company attempting to implement such a radical change. Rather than aligning its marketing with traditional market-oriented dynamics, the organization adopts a regenerative perspective, seeking to root its business activities in the functioning of living systems.   Our preliminary analysis identifies the transformation process of marketing practices and examines the organizational tensions that emerge along the way. We will present a three-stage process and the major emerging tensions.
